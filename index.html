Foreword
Some people find you. The best ones stay.

This isn't going to be one of those serious cards. You already know. This is just a little diary — four stories, a few laughs, and whatever memory I couldn't fit on one page.

Light a candle, make a wish. Then keep reading.

Chapter One
Somewhere, years ago
The day we stopped being just brothers.

You were the loud one. I was the quiet one. For years that's just how it was — two different frequencies sharing a roof.

Then something shifted. One evening we actually talked. About stupid things, then real things. You listened like it mattered, and it did.

I don't remember the date. I remember the feeling. That was the day you stopped being my sibling and became my friend.

01
Chapter One, cont.
Little things I never said.

You were the first person to treat my weird ideas like they weren't weird.

You taught me that confidence is just stepping into the room like you belong there — even on days you don't feel it.

And you always, always, had my back. Even when I was clearly in the wrong. Especially then.

02
Chapter Two
The first real night out
The night we partied like hell.

You'd been telling me for months: "one night, you and me, we're going out properly." I thought you were exaggerating. You were not.

The music was loud enough to feel in our chests. You knew everybody. Somehow, by the end of the night, so did I.

That was the night I understood why they call you ins0mniac. The city doesn't sleep when you're in it.

03
Chapter Two, cont.
Scenes I'll never forget.

— You ordering for both of us like you'd been coming here for years.

— The DJ dropping that track and you looking at me like, see? I told you.

— Walking home at some ridiculous hour, still buzzing, promising we'd do it again.

We did. Many times. Still do.

04
Chapter Three
When we had too much fun
The one where we lost track of time.

It started simple. A plan for "a couple hours." You and me and one of those moods where nothing feels like it can go wrong.

Then it became a whole day. Then a whole night. Then the sun came up and we were still laughing at something nobody else would find funny.

That's the secret, right? The best days are the ones that refuse to end.

05
Chapter Three, cont.
Why it worked.

Because we weren't trying to impress anyone. No photos for the internet. No "we should probably get going." Just — this is fun, let's keep going.

You have this thing where you can make an ordinary afternoon feel like a holiday. I still don't know how you do it.

If you ever figure it out, teach me.

06
Chapter Four
The support chapter
Every time I needed you, you showed up.

Not just the big things. The small things too. The 2 AM "hey, you good?" texts. The "I'll handle it, don't worry." The rides, the advice, the pep talks I pretended not to need.

Most people wait to be asked for help. You just notice. Then you do the thing.

I don't think I've ever properly thanked you for that. So — thank you. Thank you for being exactly the brother I needed.

07
Chapter Four, cont.
What you taught me, in one line each.

— Loyalty isn't a word, it's a schedule.

— If the people you love are good, the day was good.

— Sleep is optional. Vibes are not.

— Family is the one group chat you never mute.

08
